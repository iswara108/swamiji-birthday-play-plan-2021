Scenery: The forest

Scene 1: Introduction of Characters

Duration: 3 minutes

Over the background music: Good Morning, Peer Gynt, the five pāṇḍavas and Draupadī greet each other good morning and shortly explain the background, where and who they are.
At the end, the priest frantically knocks on the door and complains that Kāśī stole his sticks for fire. The Pāṇḍavas hastily go after her to help the priest.

Script

Yudhiṣṭhira
Oh my dear strong & ferocious brother, Bhīma, how are you on this fine day?
Bhīma
Yudhiṣṭhira, the wisest of men, my eldest brother, today I am well. After 12 years of exile in the forest we have only one year to go until we reclaim our kingdom.
Arjuna, our artistic and brave brother, trained in archery for our great victory, are you well?
Arjuna
Yes, I am very well. Every day is one day closer to the death of the wicked kauravas and for us to reclaim our kingdom with Yudhiṣṭhira rightfully on the throne.
Yudhiṣṭhira
Dear brothers, what would I do without you?
Nakula
Well you'd probably just meditate all day and forget about the kingdom.
Draupadī
Nakula, how dare you? Yudhiṣṭhira is your older brother. He is a kṣatriya, a warrior, a protector, a ruler and a righteous man. It is only right for us to get our kingdom back which was cunningly stolen from us by the kauravas in an unfair game. You all witnessed how I was humuliated by the evil Duśāsana who tried to dishonour me in front of everyone. Only Kṛṣṇa was there to protect me with his mercy and love.
Nakula
I am sorry, my fiery wife. We still have a year until we can fulfill our dharma and bring justice to our land. We are counting the days to reunite Yudhiṣṭhira with his crown.
Priest
(walks in frantically) Help, help! Kaśī took my arani, the sticks for fire. I cannot do my pūjā. I cannot do anything. She stole them, please get her, fast.

Scene 2: The chase after Kāśī - film

Duration: 2 minutes

Show a video of the chase.
The chase involves ~5 second shots alternating between Kāśī and the Pāṇḍavas chasing her, along with the music of Anrita's Dance, Peer Gynt. Each 4 bars constitue one shot. Altogether, around 11 pairs of shots. At the end, Kāśī manages to escape.

Scene 3: Lake of death - blaming the past

Duration: 2 minutes

The Pāṇḍavas helplessly give up the chase and sit together.
They enter the scene crashing down. They ask each other why this has happened to them, and each one giving some reasons, ultimtely remembering the game of dice, which is elaborated in the next scenes as a reminisce.

Script

Arjuna
What dishonour to displease a priest! How far we have fallen, what has become of us?
Bhīma
I should have killed Dusanana when he tried to remove Draupadi’s sari, we’re being punished for my action in inaction.
Sahadeva
No Brother, surely we’re being punished for when I didn’t kill Shakuni for arranging the game of dice that lost us our kingdom.
Arjuna
All of our problems started on that dark day, with that game of dice 12 years ago……

Change of scnenery to the palace, everyone in regal clothing

Scene 4: The game of dice

Duration: 5 minutes

Special setup: Śakuni has a loud speaker and the Pāṇḍavas have their cameras ready - for a guided tour

A guided tour of the palace and the new hall by Śakuni. He gives some instructions about do's and don'ts in the tour, then proceeds to tell about the new hall and the Kauravas heritage.
After the tour, Śakuni tactfully invites Yudhiṣṭhira to a game of dice, just to "nicely pass the time".
The brothers are nervous and Yudhiṣṭhira tries to escape the game, but ultimately accepts the challenge.
Śakuni shows the good dice, and hides the faulty one.
Śakuni invites Yudhiṣṭhira to put something on the stakes. Yudhiṣṭhira starts with something small (exclaiming the stakes and puts some chips on the table), and Śakuni matches the stakes, and throws the dice. "Won" he proclaims, and gathers the chips to his pile.
This process gets repeated until Yudhiṣṭhira loses his brothers, himself and their wife.
Duśāsana is sent to tell Draupadī that she is now the slave of the Kauravas.

Script

Śakuni
Ladies and losers, I mean Gentlemen, welcome to the grand tour of the new hall. Thank you for accepting our olive branch and joining us today.
Please keep your shoes off, swords in their sheaths, and masks on during the tour. Stay in the designated areas and keep your hands where I can see them.

As you can see, everything here is very expensive. On your right, you can see our beachfront deluxe rooms with two double beds and a large balcony overlooking the ocean. On your left, there is the kitchen’s secret storage. No one is allowed in there, or else, you will have to answer to the kitchen manager, explaining where the chocolate chips went. Up ahead is Atlantis which has a top class casino. Speaking of stupid decisions, how about a friendly game of dice? (Brothers have displeased looks)
Yudhiṣṭhira
I really shouldn’t, but thank you for asking.
Śakuni
What’s a game between friends? Please, accept my invitation in the name of the king.
Yudhiṣṭhira
In the name of the king, of course I must accept, although no good can come from this. (sitting at a table)
Śakuni
Here’s the dice: (shows the good dice and hides the bad one behind his back). What is your wager?
Yudhiṣṭhira
Wait, we don’t play to gamble on anything, I thought you just wanted to pass the time…
Śakuni
ahh, I can see you are attached to your new wealth.
Yudhiṣṭhira
not at all, You do not see my point. I do not believe in winning wealth by chatting. A wise man becomes a fool once he lays his hands on the dice. It robs a man of his reasoning power. It is like wine. It destroys the good qualities of a man. Once the fever gets into a man, nothing can cure him. This game is a thing to be avoided like some dread disease. Let us not play the game of dice.
Śakuni
OK, so you are challenged. Are you in or out? Tell us frankly.
Yudhiṣṭhira
I am in. You know I must not refuse a challenge as a kshatriya.
Śakuni
Very well, then. What is your wager?
Yudhiṣṭhira
I put my chariots on the stakes.
Śakuni
I put the king’s chariots against yours. “Won”
Yudhiṣṭhira
I put my jewels on the stakes
Śakuni
I put all the king’s jewels to match yours. “Won”
Yudhiṣṭhira
I put all my elephants, cows, army and treasury
Śakuni
The king will match your wager. “Won”. I am afraid you have lost all your earthly belongings, Yudhiṣṭhira. You have nothing now. If you have something that you can call yours, you can wager that. I will wager all that the prince has won so far. If you win, you can claim it all back. (silence for a moment)
Yudhiṣṭhira
I still have something to offer as my wager. This young, dark and handsome brother of mine, this Nakula, will be my wager.
Śakuni
“Won”
Yudhiṣṭhira
The wise Sahadeva is the next wager. There is no one like him in this world. I do not like the thought of using him as my wager. But I have to do it.
Śakuni
“Won”. The sons of mādrī are lost. You have two more brothers. Evidently you do not think they are good enough to be used as wagers. I think you are right. Nakula and Sahadeva are dear to you and so you used them as wagers. But actually we did not consider that the stakes were equal. Still, we could afford to be broad-minded. That is why I allowed you to use them as your wath against that of the king. Let it pass. We have won. But considering the remaining brothers, I think you are right in thinking that they are not good enough as wagers.I think so too. But you must do something if the game is to continue. Or perhaps you think that they are far superior to the sons of madri. That is perhaps the reason for your hesitation.
Yudhiṣṭhira
Please do not say such things. Your idea is to cause dissension among us. You can never do that. Here is arjuna who has no equal in the world. He is my wager.
Śakuni
“Won”
Yudhiṣṭhira
Here is Bhīma whose strength is greater than that of all of you. Bhīma is my next wager.
Śakuni
“Won”
Yudhiṣṭhira
I am the wager now.
Śakuni
“Won”. (silence) You still have draupadi. You have not lost her yet. (Bhima grasping his mace firmly to throw it at the head of sakuni. Arjuna stops him with his eyes)
Yudhiṣṭhira
Draupadi, the favoured queen of the pandavas, is my wager now.
Śakuni
“Won”

Scene 6: Final game and deportation

Duration: 4 minutes

Bhīma asks Yudhiṣṭhira what they should do, Śakuni intervenes and suggests one final tossing of the dice. If the Pāṇḍavas win - they get everything back. If they lose - they are exiled for 12 years into the forest and a 13th year in incognito. If they are discovered - another period of 12 years in exile.
The game is on. They lose.
Everything quiets down and becomes still. The music Claire de Lune, Debussy plays. The Pāṇḍavas with a heavy heart wear the tree barks, and quietly leave. Curtain closes. Music gradually quiets as the curtain slowly closes.

Bhīma
Yudhiṣṭhira, what will we do now?
Sakuni
I have a suggestion: We will have one final tossing of the dice. The winner will be the lord of the world. The loser will be exiled to the forest for 12 years and one more year in incognito. If any of the 6 of you is recognized during the final year, you will have to spend 12 more years in exile.
Yudhiṣṭhira
Agreed
Śakuni
Won, (laughing)

Sadly, starting the clair de lune and the pandavas leave.

Change of scenery to the forest and a lake

Duration: 3 minutes

The brothers briefly end the previous discussion, saying that this is what has led them to this dvaitavāna forest - the forest of duality, due to a curse. Yudhiṣṭhira sends out Nakula in search of water.
From this point on there is silence, only the background music In the Mountain Hall of the Mountain King, Peer Gynt starts playing as Nakula, Sahadeva, Arjuna and Bhīma each one goes out in search of water, looks around, sees their dead brothers (starting after Nakula dies), gets closer to the lake, hears a voice, ignores the noise, drinks and dies.
